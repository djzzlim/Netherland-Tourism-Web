The Land of Windmills and Tulips.

The newcomer will be forgiven for not knowing what to expect about the Netherlands, after tales of windmills, clogs, dikes, and tulips.

History

Also known as Holland in the past, Netherlands means 'low lands' in Dutch. Yes, you are right! Netherlands is a part of the Dutch Empire. During World War II (1939-1945), the Netherlands was invaded and occupied by the Germans (1940). After two years of relative prosperity, when only the Jewish population has been prosecuted, the whole country began to suffer the burden of war and increasing German terror. However after the difficult years of reconstruction directly after the WWII, the Netherlands sustained in the second half of the 20th century a continuous and fast economical growth. Today the Netherlands is one of the most developed and wealthiest countries in the world.


Region And Cities

Located in the northwestern of the Europe, Netherlands is surrounded by the North Sea to the north and west, Germany to the east and Belgium to the south.
It's capital city is Amsterdam while the seat of government is in The Hague.

Peoples and Language

People in Netherlands are called Dutch. Their primary language is also the Dutch language.
Some well-known people from Netherlands are

  1. Vincent van Gogh

    A famous artist from the 19th century, Vincent Willem Van Gogh was born in 30th March 1853 and died in July 29, 1890. He has created many famous painting such as:

    • The Starry Night
    • Self Potrait

  2. Johan Cruyff

    A footballer who won Ballon d'Or three times and also the founder of the football philosophy, Total Football. In the late 1960's and 1970's, he led the Netherlands to the final of the 1974 FIFA World Cup and received the Golden Ball as player of the tournament.


Climate

The average temperature in Netherlands rose by almost 2 degrees Celcius from 1906 to 2017. Netherlands also has the fourth largest CO2 emissions per capita of the European Union. This resulted in increased frequency of droughts and heatwaves. As Netherlands is very near sea-level, Netherlands is very vulnerable to sea level rise.
